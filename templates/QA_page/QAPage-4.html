  2. Algorithm Mechanisms 

  • How Does IQS Decide Relevant Articles?

    The IQS uses Elasticsearch as the main tool to search for top relevant documents that match the same keywords as you input, similar to a Google search. The IQS also uses another tool called Metamaplite to extract medical concepts from a text. Querying using these concepts can catch articles that are related to the search terms and allow for inspiration. 

  • How are the Key Sentences Extracted? 

    We use Spacy TextRank (https://derwen.ai/docs/ptr/) to generate key sentences. The TextRank can rank the relevance of each sentence. We parse the abstract of a document through the TextRank pipeline, and the top two or three relevant sentences in an article’s abstract will be highlighted to indicate a brief summary of this abstract/document. The highlighted sentence you can see before clicking the ‘expand to see’ in the search results is the top relevant sentence taken from TextRank.

  • What Does a 'Key Sentence' Mean?

    A key sentence is an important sentence of the abstract of a document. Two or three key sentences are used to summarize the long abstract, making it more convenient to figure out the main idea of an article with a quick glance. To see the full abstract, click 'Expand to See’ to view it within the IQS, or you may click ‘Full Text’ to view the abstract in a full article outside of the IQS.
